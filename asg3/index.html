Creative Brief

>Problem: The website for the comet cupboard doesn't clearly communicate the steps students need to take to be able to use the cupboard and when the cupboard is closed/open.

>Audience: The primary audience will be potential users of the cupboard, which will be mostly students and some staff. The secondary audience will be potential donors to the cupboard.

>Competitors: Wilkinson Center, Local Good Pantry, Catholic Charities Dallas

>Key message: Potential users of the comet cupboard are deterred by the confusion surrounding the user orientation and the pantry's schedule. If the website was less confusing the cupboard would see an increase in customers who can smoothly check out items and would need to throw out less un-checked out food

>Call to action: When the potential users of the cupboard see the website, they will be more likely to use the cupboard. When potential donors see the website, they will be more likely to donate to the cupboard.

>How to measure success: If more people begin using the cupboard, and if they are able to easily go through the orienation and check out items.
